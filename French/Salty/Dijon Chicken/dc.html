Dijon Chicken


Ingredients

  • 1 Â½ pounds skin-on, bone-in chicken thighs

  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt

  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

  • 1 tablespoon butter

  • 1/4 cup plus 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard, divided

  • 1 medium yellow onion, sliced

  • 3/4 cup white wine

  • 3/4 cup chicken broth

  • 1/3 cup heavy cream

  • 1 large bay leaf

  • 5 sprigs fresh thyme

How to make

    Step I
  • Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).

    • Step II
  • Sprinkle chicken thighs all over with salt and pepper. Heat oil and butter in a large, oven-proof skillet over medium-high heat. Place chicken thighs into the skillet, skin-side down. Cook until skin is golden on one side, 3 to 4 minutes; turn thighs over. Brush with 1 tablespoon mustard and continue to cook until bottom side is golden, 3 to 4 minutes more. Transfer thighs to a plate.

    • Step III
  • Reduce heat to medium; add sliced onion and remaining 1/4 cup of mustard to the skillet. Cook, stirring often, until onions are mostly translucent and evenly coated in mustard, 4 to 5 minutes. Add wine and use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from bottom of skillet. Increase heat to medium-high and bring to a boil; simmer until sauce reduces slightly and no longer smells strongly of alcohol, about 3 minutes.

    • Step IV
  • Add broth, heavy cream, bay leaf, and thyme; stir to combine. Return chicken, skin-side up, to the skillet and cover.

    • Step V
  • Bake in the preheated oven, until chicken is no longer pink at the bone and juices run clear, about 15 to 20 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted near the bone should read 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
